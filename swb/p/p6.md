# Proactive Customer Communication Strategy: Herbal Wellness
**Philosophy:** Anticipating needs before they become tickets. Focus on education, safety, and the "slow medicine" approach.

---

## 1. Empathy-Driven Email Templates

### Template A: The Shipping Delay (Supply Chain/Harvest Issues)
**Context:** When a botanical ingredient is delayed due to seasonal sourcing.
**Subject:** A note on the rhythms of the earth (Your Order #[Number])
**Body:**
"Hi [Name], 
At [Brand Name], we honor the pace of nature. To ensure the highest potency of the Elderberry in your syrup, we’ve extended our extraction process by 4 days to meet our quality standards. 
Nature isn't always on a clock, and we thank you for your patience. Your order will ship by [Date]. In the meantime, here is a digital guide on [Topic] as a thank you for your grace."

### Template B: The "How it Works" Check-in (Post-Purchase)
**Context:** Sent 10 days after delivery of a tincture/supplement.
**Subject:** How are you feeling, [Name]?
**Body:**
"Hi [Name], 
Natural shifts take time. If you’ve started your [Product Name], remember that herbal support is cumulative. It’s normal not to feel a 'jolt'—instead, look for subtle shifts in [Benefit, e.g., sleep quality].
Do you have questions about the dosage or how to integrate this into your morning ritual? Reply here; we are listening."

## 2. Chatbot Scripts (Knowledge Base Integration)

### Scenario: "Is this safe to take with my medication?"
**Bot:** "I can certainly provide our general safety guidelines! While our herbs are organic and gentle, they can be powerful. 
1. I recommend viewing our [Safety & Contraindications PDF].
2. **Crucial:** Please consult your healthcare provider before starting new supplements.
3. Would you like a list of ingredients for [Product] to show your doctor?"

### Scenario: "How do I store my dried herbs?"
**Bot:** "Great question! To keep your botanicals potent:
- Keep them in a cool, dark place (away from direct sunlight).
- Ensure the lid is airtight.
- Most of our herbs stay vibrant for 12–18 months.
Does that help, or would you like to see our 'Apothecary Storage' blog post?"
